Saint-Rémy-de-Provence

Quiet Provençal village. Walk through Glanum like a buried Roman town. Stand where Van Gogh painted Starry Night. Castle and immersive art show 15 minutes away.

Wed Jun 17 → Sat Jun 20 3 nights ~ 2 h 45 from Antibes ~ 1 h 15 to Saintes-Maries
Heads up: smaller town than Arles means fewer restaurant options and quieter evenings — that's the trade for the village charm. Also ~30 min farther from Camargue than Arles (1 h 15 vs 45 min).

Roman

Walk through an actual Roman town

★
Roman / Kids
Glanum

The standout. An excavated Greco-Roman town just south of Saint-Rémy — you walk down real streets past temples, houses, baths, fountains. Different feel from looking at an arena: more like stepping into a buried world.

Plan ~ 2 h · open daily ~9:30 am–6:30 pm · ~€8 adult, free under 18 · combo ticket with Hôtel de Sade.
Roman / Free
Les Antiques

Right at the entrance to the Glanum site, free in the open air: a Roman triumphal arch (~10 BC) and the Mausoleum of the Julii — one of the best-preserved Roman funerary monuments anywhere.

Combo with Glanum, 5 min walk apart.
Museum
Hôtel de Sade

Town museum displaying the actual artifacts excavated from Glanum — statues, inscriptions, mosaics. Smart combo right after walking the site itself.

~€3 adult · ~30–45 min.
Van Gogh

Where Starry Night was painted

★
Van Gogh / Kids
Saint-Paul-de-Mausole

The 12th-c. monastery-turned-asylum where Van Gogh stayed May 1889 – May 1890 and painted ~150 works including Starry Night, Irises, Wheatfield with Crows. You see his reconstructed room, walk the wheat-field garden he painted, look out his window. For kids who already know Starry Night, this is the trip's centerpiece moment.

Plan ~ 1 h · ~€8 adult · 10 min walk from town centre · combine with Glanum (they're next to each other).
Day trips · short drives

The Saint-Rémy radius

Geography is the hidden gift here — the Alpilles + Avignon + Arles + Pont du Gard are all under an hour.

★
Castle / Kids
Les Baux-de-Provence

Dramatic clifftop village + ruined Château des Baux you can climb on. Catapults, siege engines, panoramic views over the Alpilles. Voted one of France's most beautiful villages for a reason.

Drive ≈ 15 min · ~€11 adult, ~€9 kid · easy half-day.
★
Immersive art / Kids
Carrières de Lumières

An old bauxite quarry turned into a massive projection room — paintings 10 metres tall on the walls, set to music. You walk through the art. 2026 program: Picasso, l'art en mouvement + a Frida Kahlo short, running Feb 13 → Jul 28 (confirmed overlap with our dates). Genuine kid-wow.

Drive ≈ 15 min · at Les Baux · ~€13–16.50 adult, €12 kid · 35-min show · pair with the castle for a strong day.
History / Kids
Avignon

Palais des Papes (medieval popes' palace) and the famous half-bridge from the children's song Sur le pont d'Avignon. Big enough for a real city-feeling afternoon.

Drive ≈ 30 min.
Roman / Art
Arles

Roman arena + theatre + Van Gogh painting-trail panels. The other "big" option — you can still day-trip there from Saint-Rémy if you want both flavours.

Drive ≈ 30 min.
Roman / Water
Pont du Gard

The colossal Roman aqueduct. Swim and picnic in the Gardon below, kids' interactive space + film up top.

Drive ≈ 45 min.
In town

Saint-Rémy itself

Walk
Old town stroll

Narrow streets, hidden courtyards, fountains, Provençal honey-stone buildings. Walkable in an hour or stretched into a half-day with café stops.

Market
Wednesday morning market

One of the best in Provence — ~150 stalls fill the whole town centre. Olive oils, soaps, lavender, produce, cheese.

Catch: 8 am–1 pm Wednesdays only — we'd arrive Wed afternoon and miss it. Thursday and Friday have nothing equivalent. Plan to graze it as one of the first things Thu morning? No — it's gone.
Eat

Dinner spots

Bistros
Place de la République & Bd Mirabeau

The central square and the boulevard ringing the old town are where the bistros cluster. Pick by terrace vibe — most do Provençal classics (daube, lamb from the Alpilles, herbes-de-Provence everything).

Provençal
L'Estagnol

Long-running Saint-Rémy bistro on a shaded square — local Provençal cooking, terrace dining, kid-friendly. Worth a booking.

Pizza / Easy
Easy / kid-friendly dinner

The town has several casual pizzerias and brasseries for low-stakes dinners. Walk the old town and pick a terrace.
