Antibes & the Riviera
MON 15 – WED 17 JUNE
2 nights · arrival leg (jetlagged — keep day one easy)

⌂ Where you're staying

Charmant 3 Pièces, Vieil Antibes ↗

3 Rue des Paveurs — in the pedestrian heart of the old town.

🅿 Parking

Old town is pedestrianized — no parking at the door. Best: Q-Park Pré aux Pêcheurs (newest, fits larger cars) or open-air Parking Port Vauban (cheaper). Buy a multi-day pass. Skip Q-Park La Poste.

Options — pick a couple
Easy / Food
Vieil Antibes + Marché Provençal

Wander the old town and graze the covered Provençal market on Cours Masséna — fruit, cheese, olives, socca.

Best for a low-key jetlag arrival afternoon.
Beach
Plage de la Gravette

Sheltered sandy cove tucked against the old-town ramparts — calm, shallow, walkable.

Best for first-day swim with no driving.
★
Day trip / Boat
Île Sainte-Marguerite (Lérins)

Short ferry to a pine-forest island with swimming coves and the fort that held the "Man in the Iron Mask." Trails to roam.

Standout half-day. Ferry from Port Vauban.
Walk / Nature
Sentier du Littoral (Tirepoil)

Signed coastal path around Cap d'Antibes past secret coves and grand villas. Turn back whenever. Wear grippy shoes.

Note: can shorten to any length.
Beach
Plage de la Garoupe

Cap d'Antibes' prettiest sand beach — fine sand, clear water, mountain views.

Heads up: under renovation through ~June 2026 — check it's reopened before going.
History / Views
Fort Carré

16th-c. star-shaped fort on the harbor, 4 ha of grounds and a 43 m rampart walk with sweeping views.

Note: reservations recommended; check hours.
Culture
Picasso Museum, Château Grimaldi

Seafront castle where Picasso worked in 1946. Quick, art-forward stop for the grown-ups.

Views
Chapelle & Phare de la Garoupe

Hilltop chapel + lighthouse with panoramas over Juan-les-Pins and the bay toward Nice.

Day trip
Nice, Cannes or a hill village

If energy allows: Nice old town & flower market, Cannes' Croisette, or hilltop Èze / St-Paul-de-Vence. Save for the non-arrival day.

Drive day · Wed Jun 17
Aix-en-Provence — lunch + stroll

Right on the A8, halfway to Nîmes — zero detour. Cours Mirabeau (tree-shaded boulevard), Place Richelme daily food market, the four-dolphins fountain, gelato. Lunch + 2-hour wander = arrive Nîmes by late afternoon.

Or skip it: lazy Antibes morning (last swim at La Gravette, the market), leave around noon, direct to Nîmes by ~3 pm.
Eat
Dinner
Le Brulot

Old-town stalwart with a vaulted cellar and wood-fired oven — Provençal classics, kid-friendly pizzas. Reservations recommended.

Casual
Place du Safranier

Small cobbled square just south of the old town, several easy terraces — pick by vibe. Good plan-B if Le Brulot is full.

Lunch graze
Marché Provençal — lunch counters

Socca, pissaladière, charcuterie, cheese from the covered market stalls. Cheap, fast, easy with the kids. Best 8 am–1 pm.

Nîmes
WED 17 – SAT 20 JUNE
3 nights · you + kids (Kate working)

⌂ Where you're staying

Jolie Maison — Jardins de la Fontaine ↗

16 Rue Canteduc — northwest of centre, by the gardens; walkable to the Roman core.

🅿 Parking

You're a touch out of the hyper-centre — try street parking around Rue Canteduc first. Garage backups: Q-Park Jean Jaurès (near the gardens) or Parking Maison Carrée. Garages are tight.

In town — Roman Nîmes on foot
★
Roman / Kids
Arènes de Nîmes

One of the best-preserved Roman amphitheatres anywhere. The audioguide puts kids in a spectator's shoes for the gladiator games.

June hours ~9 am–7 pm.
Roman
Maison Carrée

An intact Roman temple in the centre, ringed by café terraces and ice-cream stops; short film inside. UNESCO-listed.

Roman / Kids
Musée de la Romanité

Sleek interactive museum facing the arena — includes Ludo, a hands-on kids' space (ages ~5–12).

Walk / Views
Tour Magne + Jardins de la Fontaine

Leafy 18th-c. gardens (by your rental) climbing to a Roman tower — 140 steps to a panorama. Great for burning kid energy.

Food
Les Halles de Nîmes

Covered market (daily 7 am–1 pm) for a build-your-own lunch — local cheeses, charcuterie, brandade, pastries.

Eat
Local specialty
La Brandade Raymond

Nîmes institution for brandade de morue (whipped salt cod). Lunch sit-down or takeaway for a picnic in the Jardins.

Terrace
Brasseries on Place de la Maison Carrée

Several easy terraces facing the Roman temple — pick by vibe. Solid kid-dinner spot with sights to watch.

Bistro
Central Nîmes bistros

The pedestrian streets between the arena and the Maison Carrée have dozens of small places — wander after the late-evening light fades.

Day trips — short drives
★
Top pick / Water
Pont du Gard

The colossal Roman aqueduct. Swim and picnic in the Gardon below, a kids' interactive space + film up top; kayaking from Collias as an add-on.

Drive ≈ 30 min · on the City Pass.
Town / Market
Uzès

Honey-stone medieval town with the Duché castle and an arcaded square.

Drive ≈ 30 min · market Wed & Sat.
Roman / Wine
Mas des Tourelles — Roman winery

Working winery in Beaucaire on a Roman villa site — reconstructed cella vinaria (Roman cellar) with demos of ancient winemaking. Wine tasting for the grown-ups, Roman-history activity for the kids. Ties to the Pont du Gard / Nîmes Roman thread.

Drive ≈ 25 min east of Nîmes.
Active / Kids
Accrobranche — Escattes Aventure

Treetop park 10 min from the centre: 14 courses (from age 2/4), ziplines, continuous safety line. €7–22. Pure kid fun.

Drive ≈ 15 min · open daily May–Jun.
History / Kids
Avignon — Palais des Papes

Palais des Papes and the famous half-bridge (Pont d'Avignon) from the children's song.

Drive ≈ 45 min.
Roman / Art
Arles

Roman arena, theatre, and the Van Gogh trail.

Plan: do it on the Sat Jun 20 drive — full details in the Camargue section below.
Camargue · Saintes-Maries
SAT 20 – WED 24 JUNE
4 nights · beach base + wildlife · the relaxed leg

⌂ Where you're staying

Maison de famille avec rooftop ↗

28 Chemin des Macreuses, Saintes-Maries-de-la-Mer.

🅿 Parking

Easiest of the three. A free, well-rated lot is on your own street — "Parking Chemin des Macreuses." Avoid the free lot at Chemin du Pont Blanc (break-in reports); never leave valuables in the car.

Camargue practicals: June runs ~28–32°C — pack sun shirts, hats, high-SPF. Saintes-Maries is famous for evening mosquitoes; bring repellent. Local shops largely close Sunday Jun 21 — grab groceries en route Saturday or first thing.
Sun Jun 21 · Fête de la Musique: free public music in every French town that evening. Saintes-Maries usually has something on the seafront; Arles is the bigger nearby scene if you want a dinner-and-music night out.
Beach & town
Beach
Plage des Saintes-Maries / l'Arène

Sandy, gently shelving beach walkable from the village — your easy default beach days.

Beach / Epic
Plage de l'Espiguette

Vast wild dune beach if you want one "wow" beach day with space to roam.

Drive ≈ 1 h (toward Le Grau-du-Roi).
History / Views
Fortified church rooftop

Climb 53 steps for a panorama over village and sea; the church Van Gogh painted, visible 10 km off. Interior free, terrace ~€3.

Kids / Nature
Le Petit Train Camarguais

50-min narrated ride through the regional park — ponds, bulls, horses, flamingos. Departs by the tourist office; €10 adult / €6 child.

Camargue nature — the local magic
★
Nature / Kids
Parc Ornithologique de Pont de Gau

Boardwalks and hides among hundreds of flamingos and other birds — the easiest wildlife win with kids. €7.50 adult / €5 child.

Drive ≈ 7 min · no booking needed.
Active / Nature
Horseback ride — Promenade Lou Seden

Top pick: a Camargue equestrian centre near the village with rides for all levels, across beach and marsh past bulls and flamingos. Calm horses, good with kids.

Book ahead in summer.
Nature
4×4 or vintage 2CV safari

Guided run deep into the wetlands for white horses, black bulls and flamingos — no driving yourself. Several operators depart from the village in 4×4s or open-top 2CVs; pick on the day from the tourist office or by Maps reviews.

Boat / Nature
Bateau Tiki III — Petit Rhône cruise

1h30 on an old paddlewheel boat up the Petit Rhône — herds and birds from the water, with a gardian/manade stop. €16 adult / €9 child.

Active
Bike the Digue à la Mer

Flat sea-dike path separating marsh from sea — rent bikes in town, flamingos overhead.

Day trips
★
History / Kids
Aigues-Mortes + salt flats

Walk the ramparts of a perfectly walled medieval town, then the rose-pink salt pans next door by little train.

Drive ≈ 40 min · strong combo day.
Wine / Sand
Domaine de Jarras / Listel

Vines growing in the dunes between Aigues-Mortes and the sea — the famous Camargue "gris de gris" rosé. Big grounds to wander, tours and tastings. Easy with kids; natural add-on to the Aigues-Mortes day.

Drive ≈ 40 min · pair with Aigues-Mortes.
★
Roman / Art / Market
Arles — the Sat Jun 20 stop

Roman arena and theatre plus the Van Gogh trail — reproduction panels next to the actual spots he painted (the yellow Café Van Gogh on Place du Forum, the "Espace Van Gogh" hospital courtyard). Fondation Vincent van Gogh has rotating shows. Huge Saturday market on Boulevard des Lices.

The drive: Nîmes check-out → 30 min to Arles → lunch, walk Van Gogh + Roman sites, dinner around Place du Forum → 45 min to Saintes-Maries. Grab groceries before leaving Arles to beat Sunday closures.
Roman / Water
Pont du Gard

If you skipped it from Nîmes, it's still reachable for a river-swim day.

Drive ≈ 1 h.
Eat
Experience
Manade lunch

A handful of manades (working bull farms) open for lunch — gardian-stew, a ranch tour, sometimes a horse demo. Manade Saint-Louis and Manade Jacques Bon are well-rated. Book ahead.

Seafood
Seafront places at the port

Casual terraces along the port — tellines (small local clams), gambas, friture. Easy walk from the rental.

Dinner
Le Brûleur de Loups

Long-running seafront restaurant — Camargue specialties, more dressed-up than the port spots but still kid-friendly.
