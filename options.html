The almost-picks

Avignon

Palais des Papes (medieval) + Pont du Gard + Orange (the best-preserved Roman theatre in Europe, 30 min). Strong on history. But city is bigger/busier, and it's not closer to Camargue than Nîmes was.

Aix-en-Provence

Most beautiful Provençal town. Cuts the Antibes drive in half. But light on Roman ruins — you'd still day-trip to Nîmes / Arles for the kids' Roman fix.

Orange

The Roman theatre is jaw-dropping. But the town itself is small, fewer dinner options, and it's ~1 h 50 from Camargue.

Split stays (e.g. 2 Arles + 1 Antibes)

Avoid. Single-night stays are check-in/out hassle without time to enjoy the place. Better to commit to one base.

Brian's read

My recommendation

Arles, unless the quieter shape calls.

Arles is the safest excellent choice — closest to "like-for-like Nîmes swap," shorter drive to Camargue, Van Gogh trail bonus, and it folds the Sat Jun 20 plan into the base. It's also the liveliest of the three (small-city Provence, not metro), which gives the trip a bit of evening variety between two quiet bases. Lowest regret.

Saint-Rémy is the equally-strong but differently-shaped pick on kid value: Glanum + the Starry-Night asylum + Les Baux 15 minutes away. The trade-off — given Antibes and Saintes-Maries are also quiet — is three sleepy bases in a row. Tiebreaker is whether we want full slow-down or a touch of small-city variety.

Extend-Antibes is the right call if either of us is craving slow-down — but means no dedicated Roman/Van Gogh base, and the whole trip stays low-key.
