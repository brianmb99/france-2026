Option 1

Arles

Top pick

Move the base to Arles

The closest thing to a like-for-like Nîmes swap. Same Roman tier, shorter drive to Camargue, Van Gogh trail as a bonus, and the Sat Jun 20 plan we already had becomes "wake up and check out."

Roman

UNESCO arena (smaller than Nîmes but same impact), Roman theatre, Cryptoporticus (underground galleries — kid-fun), Alyscamps necropolis, Thermes de Constantin. The whole circuit is walkable in an afternoon.

Van Gogh

City-run trail with reproduction panels at the actual painting spots — Café Van Gogh on Place du Forum (the yellow café), Espace Van Gogh (the hospital courtyard). Fondation Vincent van Gogh runs rotating shows.

Day trips

Pont du Gard 25 min · Nîmes 35 min (so you can still see the arena) · Les Baux + Carrières de Lumières 30 min · the Camargue starts at your doorstep.

Catch

Buzzy small city — summer Saturday market gets crowded. Mid-June heat is real (28–32 °C). Restaurants are good but not vast.

Why it could win: nothing is lost from the Nîmes plan; several things are gained (Van Gogh, ~15 min less driving to Camargue, the Sat stop comes free). Lowest-regret swap.
Option 2

Saint-Rémy-de-Provence

Charmer pick

Smaller. More concentrated. Possibly more magical for the kids.

Glanum + Van Gogh's asylum + Les Baux 15 minutes away. Quieter than Arles, but the kid-stuff is denser — and weirder-in-a-good-way.

Roman

Glanum — an actual excavated Greco-Roman town. You walk down ancient streets past temples, houses, baths. Different feel from "look at an arena from outside" — more like walking through a buried world. Plus Les Antiques right next door: a Roman triumphal arch and funerary monument.

Van Gogh

Saint-Paul-de-Mausole — the asylum where he was treated and painted Starry Night (among ~150 other works). You see his reconstructed room, walk the wheat-field garden he painted, see views from his window. For kids who already know Starry Night, this could be the trip memory.

Day trips

Les Baux-de-Provence 15 min — dramatic hilltop fortress kids can climb on. Carrières de Lumières right there: immersive art projection inside an old bauxite quarry (rotating shows; Van Gogh has been featured in past seasons — check what's running). Avignon 30 min, Arles 30 min if you want both.

Catch

Smaller town = fewer restaurant options, quieter evenings. ~30 min farther from Camargue than Arles. If you want urban buzz, this isn't it.

Why it could win: Glanum + the Starry-Night asylum + a fortress castle 15 minutes away is an unusually well-stocked menu for kids 8 and 10. Quiet evenings in a Provençal village can be the thing you remember a year later.
Option 3

Extend Antibes

Simple pick

Stay 5 nights on the Riviera. One big drive at the end.

Skip the middle move. Two extra beach days, a possible Lérins or hill-village add, then a single ~4-hour drive to Camargue with the Aix lunch we already planned.

What it adds

Two more Riviera days — slower pace, no mid-trip packing. Use them for the Île Sainte-Marguerite ferry (pine-forest island + Iron-Mask fort), a hill village (Èze or St-Paul-de-Vence), Cannes, or just more beach.

What it costs

You lose the dedicated Roman/Van Gogh base. Both become day trips from the Camargue (Arles 45 min, Nîmes 1 h, Pont du Gard 1 h 15). Doable — but you'd be using Camargue beach time to drive.

Drive math

Total driving across the trip works out the same as the current plan: instead of two ~3-hour drives, you do one ~3 h 45 drive on Sat Jun 20 with Aix-en-Provence as the lunch stop.

Booking

✓ Confirmed available — Guillaume has the place free Jun 17–20. Same Airbnb, same host, same key — just extend the existing booking by 3 nights.

Why it could win: if everyone's tired by day 3 — jetlag, heat — more beach and zero packing is a real gift. Roman becomes a Camargue day trip; perfectly fine, just less immersive.
Considered and ranked lower

The almost-picks

Avignon

Palais des Papes (medieval) + Pont du Gard + Orange (the best-preserved Roman theatre in Europe, 30 min). Strong on history. But city is bigger/busier, and it's not closer to Camargue than Nîmes was.

Aix-en-Provence

Most beautiful Provençal town. Cuts the Antibes drive in half. But light on Roman ruins — you'd still day-trip to Nîmes / Arles for the kids' Roman fix.

Orange

The Roman theatre is jaw-dropping. But the town itself is small, fewer dinner options, and it's ~1 h 50 from Camargue.

Split stays (e.g. 2 Arles + 1 Antibes)

Avoid. Single-night stays are check-in/out hassle without time to enjoy the place. Better to commit to one base.

Brian's read

My recommendation

Arles, unless the quieter shape calls.

Arles is the safest excellent choice — closest to "like-for-like Nîmes swap," shorter drive to Camargue, Van Gogh trail bonus, and it folds the Sat Jun 20 plan into the base. Lowest regret.

Saint-Rémy is the equally-strong but differently-shaped pick: Glanum + the actual Starry-Night asylum + Les Baux 15 minutes away. Quieter evenings; possibly the better kid memory. Honest tie on kid value; tiebreaker is what we want the evenings to feel like.

Extend-Antibes is the right call if either of us is craving slow-down — but only if losing the dedicated Roman/Van Gogh base doesn't bother us.
