Arles

Charmer pick

Saint-Rémy

Quieter village. Walk through Glanum (excavated Greco-Roman town), stand where Van Gogh painted Starry Night, fortress + Carrières de Lumières light show 15 min away. Denser kid-magic, sleepier evenings, ~30 min farther from Camargue than Arles.

Trade Roman/Van Gogh for the most dramatic coast in France — boat into turquoise calanques, drive Cap Canaille at sunset. The "once you see it you can't unsee it" Mediterranean. Costs: pricier base, pebble beach, Roman becomes ~1h45 day trips.

Considered and ranked lower

The almost-picks

Avignon

Palais des Papes (medieval) + Pont du Gard + Orange (the best-preserved Roman theatre in Europe, 30 min). Strong on history. But city is bigger/busier, and it's not closer to Camargue than Nîmes was.

Aix-en-Provence

Most beautiful Provençal town. Cuts the Antibes drive in half. But light on Roman ruins — you'd still day-trip to Nîmes / Arles for the kids' Roman fix.

Orange

The Roman theatre is jaw-dropping. But the town itself is small, fewer dinner options, and it's ~1 h 50 from Camargue.

Split stays (e.g. 2 Arles + 1 Antibes)

Avoid. Single-night stays are check-in/out hassle without time to enjoy the place. Better to commit to one base.

Brian's read

My recommendation

Pick by what we'd most regret skipping.

Arles or Saint-Rémy if the answer to "what would we regret skipping a year from now" is the Roman/Van Gogh thread. Both lean into what the kids are already pumped for. Arles is the safer lowest-regret pick; Saint-Rémy is the quieter, more concentrated kid-magic version (Glanum + Starry-Night asylum + Les Baux 15 min away).

Cassis if the answer is the Calanques scenery. They're a once-you-see-it-you-can't-unsee-it thing, and they don't day-trip from anywhere else on this trip. You'd trade away the lived Roman/Van Gogh experience — picking it up instead as day-trips from Camargue (Arles 45 min, Pont du Gard 1h15) — for the most dramatic coast in France as a lived base.

Extend-Antibes if the answer is slow down, more beach. Lowest-stress shape, no mid-trip move, but means letting both Roman and Calanques drop out of the trip.

The hardest tiebreaker between Arles/Saint-Rémy and Cassis: kids' anticipation of Roman ruins vs adults' once-in-a-lifetime scenery. Both genuine.
