Mid-trip option · top pick

Arles

Small-city Provence — same Roman tier as Nîmes, the Van Gogh trail at every corner, the Sat Jun 20 stop folded in for free. Lowest-regret swap.

Wed Jun 17 → Sat Jun 20 3 nights ~ 3 h from Antibes ~ 45 min to Saintes-Maries
Heads up: it's a small city, not a village — Saturday market day on Bd des Lices brings real crowds, and mid-June heat runs 28–32 °C. Restaurants are good but not metro-vast.

Roman

The Roman city, on foot

Most sites are walkable from each other. The Pass Liberté (~€16 adult, free under 18) bundles the arena + theatre + Alyscamps + cryptoporticus + a museum.

★
Roman / Kids
Arènes d'Arles

UNESCO Roman amphitheatre — smaller than Nîmes's but in active use (still hosts events). Walk the tiers, climb the medieval tower built into one corner.

~€9 adult (on Pass Liberté) · summer hours ~9 am–7 pm.
Roman
Théâtre Antique

Roman theatre, partially preserved with two iconic columns still standing. Smaller but atmospheric — summer festival venue.

Walking distance from the arena · on the Pass.
Roman / Kids
Cryptoporticus

Underground Roman galleries beneath the old forum — long, vaulted tunnels you walk through. Cool (literally) in the heat and a kid-favourite for the spooky-tunnel factor.

On the Pass · ~30 min visit.
Roman
Alyscamps

Roman necropolis — an alley of stone sarcophagi leading to a ruined church. Atmospheric and quieter than the central sites. Van Gogh and Gauguin both painted here.

On the Pass · 15 min walk from centre.
Museum / Roman
Musée Arles Antique

The "blue museum" — extraordinary collection of Roman finds: a barge raised from the Rhône, mosaics, Caesar bust found in the river in 2007. Worth the ~15 min walk.

On the Pass · 1.5–2 h.
Van Gogh

Walk where he painted

★
Van Gogh / Walk
Van Gogh painting-trail

City-run trail with ~10 reproduction panels at the actual locations he painted — you stand where Vincent stood. Free, self-guided, walkable in 2 hours.

Van Gogh
Café Van Gogh

The yellow café on Place du Forum — the actual setting of Café Terrace at Night. Repainted yellow to match the painting. Still operates as a café (touristy but worth a coffee for the moment).

Van Gogh / Free
Espace Van Gogh

Courtyard of the former Hôtel-Dieu hospital where he was treated after the ear incident. Garden restored to match his painting of it. Free to enter.

Van Gogh / Museum
Fondation Vincent van Gogh

Modern museum (opened 2014) with rotating exhibitions — usually at least one original Van Gogh work loaned in. Check current show before visiting.

~€10 adult.
Other

The rest of Arles

Market
Saturday market — Bd des Lices

One of the biggest markets in Provence — hundreds of stalls along the boulevard. Saturday is your check-out day; grab an hour in the morning before the drive to Saintes-Maries.

Sat 7 am–1 pm.
Romanesque
Saint-Trophime + cloister

12th-c. Romanesque church on Place de la République (the main square) with an extraordinary carved cloister. UNESCO, on the Pass Liberté.

Day trips · short drives

The Arles radius

★
Roman / Water
Pont du Gard

The colossal Roman aqueduct. Swim and picnic in the Gardon below, interactive museum + film up top.

Drive ≈ 25 min.
Castle + Art
Les Baux + Carrières de Lumières

Clifftop fortress village with castle ruins kids climb on, plus the Carrières de Lumières (immersive art projection in a quarry) right next to it. Strong combo afternoon.

Drive ≈ 30 min.
Roman
Nîmes

The other Roman city — arena, Maison Carrée temple, Musée de la Romanité. Doable as a day trip if you want both Arles and Nîmes Roman flavours.

Drive ≈ 35 min.
Medieval / Kids
Avignon

Palais des Papes + the famous half-bridge (Pont d'Avignon from the children's song).

Drive ≈ 30 min.
Eat

Dinner spots

Terrace
Place du Forum bistros

The heart of Arles dining — bistros wrap the square (including the yellow Café Van Gogh). Pick a terrace and watch the evening pass.

Bistro
Le Galoubet

Long-running Provençal bistro a couple of blocks from Place du Forum — local cooking, kid-friendly, worth booking for dinner.

Local
Camargue cuisine

Try local Camargue specialties before you arrive at the actual Camargue: gardian de taureau (bull stew), tellines (small clams), red rice from the marshes. Many Arles bistros do this.
