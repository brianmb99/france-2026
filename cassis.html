Cassis & the Calanques

Live next to the most dramatic coast in France. Boat into turquoise calanques, drive the Route des Crêtes at sunset, swim in cliff-walled coves. The "once you see it you can't unsee it" Mediterranean.

Wed Jun 17 → Sat Jun 20 3 nights ~ 2 h from Antibes ~ 2 h to Saintes-Maries
Heads up: beaches are pebble, not sand. Cassis is small and gets very crowded in summer — parking is notoriously hard. Roman/Van Gogh shifts to day-trips from the Camargue leg (Arles is 45 min from Saintes-Maries) — eating into our beach days.

The Calanques

The reason you'd come here

Limestone cliffs into turquoise water. Boat in, hike in, or swim in. Some access is restricted in summer for fire/crowd reasons — worth checking before the day.

★
Boat / Kids
Calanques boat tour

Easiest way to see them with kids — multiple operators run from Cassis port. Options range from 45 min (3 calanques) to 2 h (8+ calanques, including En-Vau). Some include swim stops. Book in advance in summer.

~€20–35 adult, kids half-ish.
Walk / Easy
Port-Miou (walkable from town)

Closest calanque, ~25 min walk from Cassis port along a flat trail. Tucked, marina-feel, fewer crowds. Good first taste of what the others are like.

Free · easy with kids.
Hike / Swim
Port-Pin

~45 min walk past Port-Miou — small pine-fringed cove, swimmable, less developed. Solid kid-doable hike with a swim reward at the end.

Free · plan for early morning in June heat.
The icon · long hike
En-Vau (the famous one)

The poster-child calanque — towering white cliffs, jewel-blue water. From Cassis it's a 2 h hike each way (rocky, exposed). Realistically: see it from the boat tour with the kids.

Hike with kids 8/10: doable but hot, not for June afternoon.
Drive / Sunset
Route des Crêtes ★

Clifftop road between Cassis and La Ciotat with Cap Canaille — one of the highest sea cliffs in France. Pullouts every few minutes for jaw-dropping views over the calanques. Best at sunset.

~1 h round trip · do it as an after-dinner light drive.
Summer access: the most famous Marseille-side calanques (Sormiou, Morgiou) close to cars in summer due to fire risk — shuttle or hike only, and capped daily entries. From a Cassis base, you stick mostly to the Cassis-side calanques (Port-Miou, Port-Pin, En-Vau) which don't have that restriction — but the boat tours are the easy answer either way.
Cassis itself

The town

Walk
Old port stroll

Pastel houses around a working fishing harbour, lined with restaurants. Walk it morning (boats coming in) and evening (terraces full). 20 min end to end.

Beach
Plage de la Grande Mer

Town beach — pebbly, sheltered, walkable from the port. Kid-fine for swimming but bring water shoes (pebble underfoot is harsh).

Wine
Cassis AOC tasting

Cassis has its own appellation, mostly whites and rosés (think marsanne, clairette). Several small domaines on the hillsides north of town offer tastings — Domaine du Bagnol and Clos Sainte-Magdeleine are well-known.

Book ahead.
Day trips

What's around

Roman / Van Gogh — not from here. Don't try to day-trip Arles, Avignon, or Pont du Gard from Cassis — they're ~1h45 each way and you'd sacrifice a Cassis day to bake in midday heat. Shift them to the Camargue leg instead, where they're 45 min – 1h15 from Saintes-Maries. The cards live on the main packet's Camargue section.
Big city / Museum
Marseille — MUCEM + Vieux Port

MUCEM (Museum of European & Mediterranean Civilisations) is interactive, modern, kid-friendly. Combine with Vieux Port, Notre-Dame de la Garde (the basilica on the hill with views over the city), and maybe Le Panier.

Drive ≈ 30 min · easy full day.
Town
Aix-en-Provence

Quintessential Provençal town — Cours Mirabeau, fountains, Place Richelme food market. Easy lunch + 2-hour stroll.

Drive ≈ 45 min.
Town
La Ciotat

Just east — birthplace of cinema (the Lumière brothers' first film was shot at the train station here in 1895). Working port, sandy beaches if you want a break from pebbles.

Drive ≈ 15 min · or via the Route des Crêtes.
Wine
Bandol

Famous Mourvèdre-based reds and rosés. Several domaines welcome visitors. Pair with lunch in the town of Bandol or Cassis on the way back.

Drive ≈ 30 min.
Eat

Dinner spots

Port terraces
Port-side bistros

The harbour is ringed with restaurants — seafood, Provençal bistro food, pizza, watching boats come and go. Pricier than inland Provence; the location is the premium.

Local classic
Bouillabaisse (one night)

The Marseille-Cassis coast's signature fish stew. Order it once during the stay. Several Cassis restaurants do a respectable version — kids may find it odd, but the broth-and-bread part is approachable.

Easy / Family
Casual / kid-friendly

The pedestrian streets behind the port have lower-key places — pizza, simple bistro food. Easier wins for kid-dinners than the port-front terraces.

Cassis · 3 nights · the scenery pick

Calanques + Cap Canaille + Cassis wine. Costs: pricier accommodations, pebble beaches, Roman/Van Gogh becomes long day trips.
